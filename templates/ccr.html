Welcome to the World of Chess Formulae

The Class Chess Rating (CCR) is calculated using a performance-centric formula, ensuring a fair and representative ranking for participants.

The CCR formula is designed to prioritize performance, while incorporating other tournament-related factors.

CCR Formula:
The CCR for a single tournament is given by:

CCRraw = (W/5) × {k1.Perf2 + k2.P + k3.R + (10/Ptl)}

  • W is weight of tournaments (1 to 5), reflecting importance
  • Perf is tournament performance score (by Glick-o or FIDE rules)
  • P is number of points scored in tournament
  • R is win-rate of the participant
  • Ptl is percentile rank

Sigmoidal Decay:
The CCR decays over time using a sigmoidal function, ensuring gradual deterioration initially, followed by a sharper decline. The decay function is:

Decay D(t) = 1/(1 + e-(t-t0))

  • t is the time elapsed since tournament in days

  • t0 is the threshold time before which rating decay is gradual, and post which rating decay is accelerated

Final CCR is given by:

CCRfinal = CCRraw.D(t)
